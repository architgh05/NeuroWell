🧠 Alzheimer’s Disease

Alzheimer’s disease is a progressive neurological disorder that causes the brain to shrink (atrophy) and brain cells to die. It is the most common cause of dementia, a continuous decline in thinking, behavioral and social skills that disrupts a person's ability to function independently.

  • It develops slowly and gets worse over time.
  • Typically affects people aged 65 and older, but early-onset Alzheimer's can appear as early as the 40s or 50s.
  • It accounts for 60-80% of all dementia cases.

⚙️ Causes

  • Biological and Neurological Changes: Amyloid plaques, neurofibrillary tangles, and neuron loss.
  • Genetic Factors: Family history, APOE-e4 gene, and rare mutations (APP, PSEN1, PSEN2).
  • Environmental & Lifestyle: Head injuries, hypertension, poor diet, smoking, alcohol, and social isolation.

🚨 Symptoms

  • Early-Stage: Memory loss, word-finding issues, disorganization, misplacing items, time/place confusion.
  • Middle-Stage: Forgetfulness, identity confusion, personality changes, sleep issues, wandering, daily task difficulties.
  • Late-Stage: Severe memory loss, loss of self-care abilities, speech loss, immobility, and infections.

🧪 Diagnosis

  • Medical history and physical exam
  • Neurological testing
  • Mental status tests (MMSE or MoCA)
  • Brain imaging (MRI, CT, PET)
  • Blood tests
  • CSF analysis (in certain cases)

💊 Treatment

  • Medications:
    • Cholinesterase inhibitors (Donepezil, Rivastigmine)
    • Memantine
    • Antidepressants or antipsychotics for behavioral issues
  • Supportive Therapies: Memory training, occupational therapy, support groups
  • Experimental Treatments: Aducanumab (Aduhelm), Lecanemab, ongoing clinical trials targeting amyloid and tau proteins

🛡️ Prevention and Risk Reduction

  • 🏃‍♀️ Regular physical activity
  • 🥗 Balanced Mediterranean-style diet
  • 🧩 Cognitive training and mental engagement
  • 🧘‍♂️ Stress management
  • 🫂 Staying socially active
  • 🛌 Getting quality sleep
  • 🚭 Avoiding smoking and limiting alcohol
  • ❤️ Managing diabetes, hypertension, and obesity

👪 Caregiving and Support

  • Ensure a safe and secure environment
  • Establish consistent routines
  • Communicate calmly and patiently
  • Explore respite or professional memory care when needed
